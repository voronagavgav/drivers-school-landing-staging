**Date:** 2026-07-20
**For:** Drivers School landing (lp v36 «Проспект»), first two folds
**Question studied:** How do excellent real websites present PROOF / CREDIBILITY / KEY NUMBERS in or near the hero?
**Method:** Live WebFetch of each landing page (markdown extraction), WebSearch fallback where markup was an SPA shell or blocked (Duolingo, Revolut), and real-browser screenshots (`agent-browser`) of the four most instructive heroes, saved under `scratchpad/lp36/refs/`.

**HARD RULE (governs this whole doc):** Everything below is abstracted to the *WHY*. ## Per-site one-liners

**driving-tests.org** (category) — *Screenshot: `refs/driving-tests-hero.png`.* Proof lives INSIDE the hero as colored number-badges pinned onto photos of real people holding their actual licenses ("97% industry-leading pass rate", "2,900 tests completed today", "2026 updated"), plus a quiet inline stat line under the CTA ("4.5M+ practice tests taken this year · State-specific · No registration required") and a press-logo band directly below. Credible because each number sits on a human who visibly achieved the outcome; the live "today" counter signals a system in motion. PRINCIPLE: **anchor a proof number to visible evidence of the outcome it claims, so the figure reads as a caption on reality rather than an assertion.**

**vodiy.ua** (category, direct UA competitor) — *Screenshot: `refs/vodiy-hero.png`.* Proof appears as small floating "chips" beside the hero image, each pairing a figure with a plain label ("117 білетів по 20 питань", "65 тем", "20/20 Ваш результат", "База МВС Офіційні питання"); a shield line under the CTA asserts the official ГСЦ МВС database; user-scale numbers (1.1M користувачів, 256M відповідей, 15+ років) are deferred to a lower band. Credible because the near-hero numbers describe *content scope and official provenance* (checkable) rather than boasting reach. PRINCIPLE: **near the hero, prefer numbers a skeptic can verify (scope, provenance, coverage) over numbers they must take on faith (reach), and reserve the boastful totals for later.**

**zutobi.com** (category) — Hero carries NO figures at all; credibility is delegated entirely to a press-logo strip immediately under the headline, and content volume is described only qualitatively ("hundreds"). Reads competent but slightly hollow up top — borrowed authority with nothing self-evidently true beside it. PRINCIPLE: **borrowed credibility (logos) without a single self-owned, concrete fact beside it feels like deflection; pair external endorsement with at least one number you own.**

**duolingo.com** (gamified learning) — *Screenshot: `refs/duolingo-efficacy.png`.* On the marketing surface, headline is pure positioning; the heavy numbers (100M+ monthly users, billions of exercises) sit in dedicated bands and a science/efficacy surface where each figure is framed as a measurable OUTCOME ("5 university semesters' worth", "improved seniors' brain health") on checkmark bullets under "top results". Credible because the number answers "what will this do for me" not "how big are we". PRINCIPLE: **convert a raw statistic into the learner's outcome — the persuasive unit is result-per-user, not size-of-company.**

**brilliant.org** (gamified learning) — *Screenshot: `refs/brilliant-hero.png`.* Hero itself embeds a LIVE interactive mini-lesson (the product literally working, right there) with zero numbers; a clean three-cell stat band ("Award-winning", "100,000+ 5-star reviews", "10 million+ learners") sits immediately below as the first scroll reward. Credible because you *see the product being good* before you're told it's popular. PRINCIPLE: **let the product demonstrate quality in the hero and let numbers confirm it one beat later; proof-by-showing outranks proof-by-counting.**

**headspace.com** (consumer app) — Hero is purely emotional (headline + one CTA); every metric ("4,000+ organizations", "1,000+ exercises", research claims) is deferred many folds down. Works only because the brand is already famous — for an unknown brand this delay would read as "no evidence". PRINCIPLE: **the height at which you can withhold proof is proportional to how much prior trust you already own; an unknown brand must front-load a concrete fact.**

**revolut.com** (consumer app; fetch blocked, via WebSearch) — Leads with a single dominant human/lifestyle claim and one flagship trust unit (customer-count milestone in the 70M+ range, Trustpilot ~4.7) treated as a headline-scale fact rather than a fine-print stat. Credible because one big number is given the visual weight of a headline instead of being diluted across a row. PRINCIPLE: **one hero-scale number given headline weight out-persuades a crowded row of small ones; pick the single most impressive true fact and let it breathe.**

**monobank.ua** (consumer app, UA) — Hero is product-shot + playful line, no metrics; even a "live statistics" feature is teased without showing figures near the top; real scale numbers live far down. Same fame-dependent deferral as Headspace, localized. PRINCIPLE: **teasing that data exists ("live stats") without showing a value spends curiosity without paying trust; show the number or don't raise it.**

## Synthesis — distilled transferable PRINCIPLES

1. **Anchor each proof number to visible evidence of the very outcome it claims, so the figure reads as a caption on something real rather than a bare assertion.**
   *Why:* a number floating in empty space is a claim; a number sitting on the thing it describes is testimony. — *evidenced by driving-tests.org, brilliant.org*

2. **Near the hero, favor numbers a skeptic can verify (scope, coverage, official provenance, freshness) over numbers taken on faith (total reach), and defer the boastful totals to a later band.**
   *Why:* checkable facts survive a doubting first read; reach claims only land once some trust is already banked. — *evidenced by vodiy.ua, driving-tests.org*

3. **Translate a raw statistic into the user's own outcome — the persuasive unit is result-per-person, not size-of-company.**
   *Why:* "how big we are" flatters the brand; "what you will get" answers the visitor's actual question. — *evidenced by duolingo.com, headspace.com*

4. **Let the product demonstrate its quality first and let numbers merely confirm it a beat later; showing beats counting.**
   *Why:* seeing the thing be good is self-authenticating, whereas a count still has to be believed. — *evidenced by brilliant.org, driving-tests.org*

5. **Give one hero-scale fact headline weight rather than diluting attention across a dense row of small figures.**
   *Why:* a single number sized like a promise is remembered; five equal-weight numbers cancel each other into noise. — *evidenced by revolut.com*

6. **Pair any borrowed credibility (press logos, official-body affiliation) with at least one concrete fact you own; endorsement alone reads as deflection.**
   *Why:* logos say "others vouch for us" but dodge the question of whether the product itself is good — one self-owned truth closes that gap. — *evidenced by zutobi.com (absence), vodiy.ua (does it)*

7. **Signal that the system is alive — a moving/"today"/recent figure — because motion implies other people are here right now, which static totals do not.**
   *Why:* a live counter converts abstract popularity into present-tense social presence, the strongest herd cue. — *evidenced by driving-tests.org*

8. **Calibrate how high you can withhold proof to how much prior fame you hold: an unknown brand must front-load one concrete fact; only a famous one can open on pure emotion.**
   *Why:* trust deferred is trust assumed, and an unknown brand has none to assume. — *evidenced by headspace.com & monobank.ua (fame lets them defer), zutobi.com (borrowing instead)*

## ANTI-patterns observed (what reads as hollow / template)

- **Logos-as-a-substitute-for-substance.** A press/partner strip with no self-owned fact beside it deflects the "is it actually good?" question instead of answering it; it feels like borrowed clothes. *(zutobi.com up top)*

- **Teasing data you don't show.** Announcing "live statistics" or "research-backed" near the top without a single actual value spends the visitor's curiosity and returns nothing — worse than staying silent. *(monobank.ua's "жива статистика", headspace's uncited "research")*

- **Undifferentiated stat rows.** Three-to-five equal-size numbers in a tidy row is the universal SaaS template; because every figure carries the same weight, none carries meaning, and the band scans as decoration. *(the generic risk brilliant.org/vodiy.ua skirt by varying weight or anchoring to imagery)*

- **Vanity totals with no per-user payoff.** A giant reach number ("N million users") with no translation into what the visitor personally gets flatters the company and leaves the reader's real question unanswered. *(the failure mode duolingo's efficacy framing deliberately avoids)*
